# Terms and jargon decoded (recruiter + general hiring)

Short definitions + **how you might use the word** in a sentence.

---

## B2B (business-to-business)

**Meaning:** The **customers are companies** (or teams inside companies), not random consumers. Sales cycles, permissions, reliability, and integrations often matter more.

**Say:** “I’m drawn to **B2B** because features tend to be **long-lived** and **deep** — workflows, permissions, integrations.”

---

## B2C (business-to-consumer)

**Meaning:** The **end user is a person** buying or using for themselves (many consumer apps).

**Say:** “Trackd is closer to **B2C** in audience, but the **engineering problems** — data, integrations, reliability — overlap with **B2B**.”

---

## Platform (especially “B2B platform”)

**Meaning:** A **core product** other workflows build on — users, orgs, billing, APIs, admin tools, sometimes an “app marketplace.”

**Say:** “When you say **platform**, I hear **shared foundation** — auth, data model, APIs — that many customers rely on.”

---

## SaaS (software as a service)

**Meaning:** Software **hosted by the vendor**, customers pay **subscription** (usually), log in via browser.

**Say:** “I’m used to **SaaS-style** delivery — **hosted** app, continuous updates, **subscription** mindset.”

---

## Full stack

**Meaning:** Engineer who works on **front end and back end** (and often cares about **deployment**).

**Say:** “I’m **full stack** with a tilt toward **TypeScript** and **product shipping**.”

---

## Backend

**Meaning:** Server, database, integrations — **not** the pixels in the browser.

**Say:** “I want more **backend** responsibility — **APIs**, **data**, **integrations**.”

---

## Frontend

**Meaning:** What the user **sees and clicks** in the browser or app.

**Say:** “I’m comfortable in the **frontend** — **React/Next** — but I do not want to be **only** UI.”

---

## Systems architecture / “architecture”

**Meaning:** **How pieces fit together** — databases, services, queues, caching, auth, scaling, failure handling. Not “which React component.”

**Say:** “I’m building **architecture** instincts from **owning Trackd** — how **auth**, **data**, and **integrations** connect, and what breaks in the real world.”

---

## API (application programming interface)

**Meaning:** **Programmatic access** — machines talking to machines using defined requests and responses.

**Say:** “I build and **consume APIs** — between my app layers and third parties.”

---

## Integration

**Meaning:** Connecting your product to **external systems** — email, CRM, payments, identity, AI providers.

**Say:** “A lot of my value is **integration** work — **email**, **CRMs**, external **APIs**.”

---

## Remote (100% remote)

**Meaning:** No default office expectation; communication is **async + video + docs**.

**Say:** “**Remote** works well for me — I over-communicate in writing and keep **overlap hours** clear.”

---

## Time zone / international project

**Meaning:** Team or customers spread globally; **meeting windows** and **handoffs** need discipline.

**Say:** “I’m used to coordinating across **time zones** and keeping **written** decisions so handoffs work.”

---

## Stakeholder

**Meaning:** Anyone with **interest** in the outcome — PM, designer, client, support, sales.

**Say:** “I stay aligned with **stakeholders** through **short updates** and **clear demos**.”

---

## MVP (minimum viable product)

**Meaning:** **Smallest** version that proves value and learns from users.

**Say:** “I think in **MVPs** — smallest slice that is **real**, **measurable**, and **safe** to ship.”

---

## Technical debt

**Meaning:** Shortcuts that **slow future work** until paid back (refactors, tests, docs).

**Say:** “I try to balance **speed** with **contained debt** — if I take a shortcut, I note **what we owe** later.”

---

## Monolith vs microservices

**Meaning:**

- **Monolith** — One main codebase/deploy unit; simpler ops early.
- **Microservices** — Many small services; flexibility and **more** operational complexity.

**Say:** “My projects are mostly **monoliths** with **clear module boundaries**.”

---

## CI/CD (continuous integration / deployment)

**Meaning:** Automated **tests/builds** on each change and automated **deploy** pipelines.

**Say:** “I rely on **hosted git** and platform deploys — I’m learning deeper **CI/CD** as needed.”

---

## Observability / monitoring (light touch)

**Meaning:** **Logs**, **metrics**, **traces** so you can see failures in production.

**Say:** “On solo projects I use **logging** and provider dashboards; in a team I’d follow whatever **observability** stack you use.”

---

## SLA / SLO (usually later-round)

**Meaning:** Promised **uptime** or performance targets.

**Say:** “I have not owned **SLAs** at enterprise scale; I understand they drive **reliability investments**.”

Only if it comes up.

---

## “Senior” vs “Mid” (recruiter vocabulary)

Roughly: **scope of ownership** — ambiguity handled, mentoring, cross-team impact. Titles vary by company.

**Say:** “I’m strongest as an **individual contributor** shipping **end to end**; I grow seniority by taking **bigger ambiguous problems**.”

---

## Staff / principal (do not claim if untrue)

Very experienced IC track — architecture across teams. Skip unless accurate.

---

## Equity / compensation bands

**Meaning:** Salary + sometimes **stock**. Recruiters often ask **expectations** early.

**Say:** “I’d like to understand your **range** for the level you have in mind.”

---

## Notice period

**Meaning:** How much time you must give your current employer before leaving.

**Say:** “My notice period is **X weeks**; I can be flexible on **start date**.”

Fill in X truthfully.

---

## Contract vs full-time (W2 vs 1099, US-ish)

**Meaning:** Employment type affects **benefits** and **taxes**. Answer what is true for you.

---

## Security / compliance (light)

**Meaning:** GDPR, SOC2, etc. — rules about **data handling**.

**Say:** “I follow **least privilege** for keys and **never log secrets**; happy to learn your **compliance** requirements.”

---

## DevOps / SRE (often blurred)

**Meaning:** People/orientation focused on **deploy, reliability, infra**. You might not be this person; that is fine.

**Say:** “I’m not a dedicated **SRE**, but I care about **healthy deploys** and **rollback paths**.”
