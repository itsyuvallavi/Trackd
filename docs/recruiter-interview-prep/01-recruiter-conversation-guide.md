# Recruiter conversation guide

## What this call usually is

A recruiter screen is mostly:

- **Fit** — Are you in the right ballpark (stack, seniority, remote, salary band)?
- **Clarity** — Can you explain what you have done without rambling?
- **Motivation** — Do you sound interested in *this* kind of work?
- **Logistics** — Next steps, timing, visa, timezone, notice period.

They are **not** trying to trick you with algorithms. If they ask something technical, they often want to hear that you **recognize the words** and have **touched that area**, not that you recite textbook definitions.

## How to speak (simple rules)

1. **Short first, details on request** — Answer in 30–60 seconds, then pause. “Happy to go deeper if useful.”
2. **One example per claim** — “I work with APIs — for example on Trackd the app talks to Supabase and to LLM providers for interview prep.”
3. **Plain language, then optional jargon** — “I store user data in a proper database — Postgres — and I use a tool called Prisma so my TypeScript code talks to the database safely.”
4. **Honest gaps** — “I have not worked on a huge B2B billing system, but I have shipped integrations and data-backed features end to end.”

## Typical flow of the call


| Phase           | What happens                      | What you do                                       |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Warm-up         | Small talk, how are you           | Brief, positive, pivot to substance               |
| Your background | “Walk me through your resume”     | Use the script in `02-your-experience-scripts.md` |
| Role overview   | They describe the project         | Listen, take notes, nod                           |
| Your questions  | They invite questions             | Ask 2–4 from the list below                       |
| Next steps      | Another interview, homework, etc. | Confirm email, timeline                           |


## What to say: “Tell me about yourself” (template)

> “I’m a software engineer focused on **TypeScript** and **shipping product end to end** — UI, data, and integrations. My main personal product right now is **Trackd**, a **Next.js** app with **Postgres** through **Prisma** and **Supabase**, plus email and extension-based capture, and some **AI-assisted** features. I also do **client work** — production sites and **automations** that connect email, spreadsheets, and CRMs. Recently I’ve been helping maintain a **Flutter** app in production. I’m looking for a **remote** role where I can go deeper on **backend and integrations** as part of a real product team.”

Adjust any sentence that is not true for you.

## What to say: “Why this role?”

Pick ingredients that match their message (B2B, APIs, architecture, remote):

> “The role lines up with what I already do — **APIs**, **TypeScript**, **data-backed features**, and **integrations** — but at a level where **architecture and reliability** matter for many customers, not just one app. I want that next step, **100% remote** works for me, and I’m excited by **B2B platforms** because the problems tend to be deep and long-lived.”

## Questions you can ask them (pick a few)

- What **team** would I join, and who would I work with day to day (engineers, PM, design)?
- What **stack** is the team mainly using today?
- What does **success in the first three months** look like for this hire?
- How do they handle **time zones** and **communication** on an international project?
- What are the **next steps** after this call, and what is the **timeline**?

## If you blank out

Say one of these and breathe:

- “Let me give you the short version first…”
- “The clearest example I have is from Trackd…”
- “I have not done that at massive scale, but here is the closest thing I have shipped…”

## After the call

Send a **short thank-you email** (same day if possible): one paragraph, restate interest, mention one specific thing you liked about what they shared, confirm you are open to next steps.
