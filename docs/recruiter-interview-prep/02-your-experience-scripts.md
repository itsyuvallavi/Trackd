# Your experience — what to say (scripts)

Use these **out loud** until they feel natural. You do not need to memorize word for word; keep the **facts** accurate.

---

## Trackd (your strongest “full stack + data + integrations” story)

### One sentence

> “Trackd is a job-application tracker I built as a **full-stack TypeScript app** — **Next.js** on the web, **Postgres** for data, with **email** and a **browser extension** so saving a role is fast, plus **AI** for things like interview prep.”

### If they want a bit more (45–60 seconds)

> “Users move applications through a **pipeline** — saved, applied, interviews, outcomes. Behind that is a **real database** with a structured **data model**, not just local storage. I integrated **email** so job-related messages can surface **in context** per application, and a **Chrome extension** for one-click capture from job sites. For **AI**, I call external **APIs** with some **guardrails** around prompts and user content. I own the stack from **schema** to **UI**.”

### Words you might define if they look confused

- **Pipeline / Kanban** — “Like a board: cards move through stages.”
- **Data model** — “The structured way the app stores jobs, users, and activities in the database.”
- **Integration** — “The app talking to something outside itself — email provider, AI API, extension talking to the page.”

---

## Metodo — Flutter (short, confident)

### One sentence

> “I’m maintaining a **production Flutter app** for iOS and Android — dependency updates, UI fixes, stability, and getting releases out without breaking existing users.”

### If they ask “Isn’t that Dart, not TypeScript?”

> “Yes, the app is **Dart/Flutter**, but the work is the same discipline — **production releases**, **regression risk**, **parity across platforms**. My **deepest recent web stack** is still **TypeScript/Next** on Trackd and client projects.”

---

## Freelance (B2B-friendly framing)

### One sentence

> “I ship **production websites** and **internal automations** — connecting **forms**, **email**, **spreadsheets**, and **CRMs** so small teams stop doing manual handoffs between tools.”

### If they say “B2B experience?”

> “A lot of my freelance work is **business-facing** — not consumer marketing sites only — **workflows and integrations** that support how a company actually operates day to day.”

## “What are you looking for?”

> “A **remote** product engineering role where I can keep **shipping end to end** but spend more time on **backend**, **APIs**, and **system design** as part of a team — not only solo projects.”

---

## “Greatest strength”

Pick one; say it simply.

- **Ownership** — “I take a feature from idea to production — UI, data, integrations, and follow-up fixes.”
- **Communication** — “I work well with **design and product**, write down decisions, and translate specs into shippable slices.”
- **Integrations** — “I’m comfortable where the app meets **real-world systems** — email, CRMs, third-party APIs — including failure cases.”

---

## “Biggest weakness” (keep it safe and real)

Avoid fake weaknesses (“I care too much”). Pick something true and show mitigation.

**Example shape:**

> “I have shipped a lot as a **generalist**. In a larger team I want to keep **deepening backend and architecture** — I’m actively studying patterns for **scalability** and **reliability** beyond what a solo product requires.”

---

## Salary / rate (if they ask)

You do not owe a number in the first five minutes. Options:

- “I’m flexible depending on **scope, level, and benefits** — what **range** is budgeted for this role?”
- If you must give a number, give a **range** you researched and can defend, then stop talking.

This doc is not legal or negotiation advice; adjust to your market and comfort.
