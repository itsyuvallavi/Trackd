# Atlana — company brief (for your conversation)

Sources: [Atlana on LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/atlana-nearshore/), public pages on [atlana.pt](https://www.atlana.pt/). Details can change; use this as orientation, not a fact sheet to recite.

---

## What kind of company this is

**Atlana is a nearshore IT services company based in Portugal.** In plain terms: they help other businesses scale delivery by providing **engineers and teams** that work **integrated with the client** — often described as **“Team as a Service” (TaaS)**, plus **project-based** help and **managed IT services**.

**“Nearshore”** means: talent in a **nearby region** (here, Portugal / Europe) so **time zones and collaboration** are easier than with very distant outsourcing.

LinkedIn currently shows a **small team** (~9 employees on the company page). That usually means **they are the partner**, and the **“international B2B platform”** from the recruiter message is very likely a **client engagement** you would staff — not necessarily a product branded “Atlana.”

From their public messaging, they emphasize:

- **Agile** nearshore teams in **Portugal**
- Depth in **AI**, **infrastructure**, and **software development**
- **Time zone alignment** and fitting in **seamlessly** with the client
- **Partnership** — trust, transparency, collaboration (not “ticket factory” language)

## Services vocabulary (so words on their site don’t surprise you)

| Term | Plain meaning |
|------|----------------|
| **TaaS (Team as a Service)** | A **dedicated team** (or squad) from the partner that works **inside the client’s rhythm** — standups, backlog, tools — rather than a black-box “we’ll email you a zip file.” |
| **Dedicated developers / staff augmentation** | **You** are assigned full-time (or mostly) to **one client**; the consultancy handles HR, payroll, bench, sometimes laptops. |
| **Managed services** | Ongoing **operations** — keeping systems running, monitoring, support — broader than “only feature development.” Their site mentions people, process, tools, infrastructure. |
| **Custom software / consultancy** | Building or modernizing **products and platforms** for clients. |

## What this implies for *your* interview

1. **Clarify the shape of work** — Is the role **full-time on one end client**, internal Atlana product work, or **multiple clients** over time?
2. **Clarify employer of record** — Who signs your contract, who manages performance reviews, who handles **time off** and **equipment**?
3. **Clarify time zones** — Portugal is **WET/WEST**; US West Coast is far behind. **“100% remote”** can still mean **core hours overlap** expectations. Ask explicitly.
4. **Clarify the “B2B platform”** — **Client name** (if they can share), **industry**, **stage** (greenfield vs legacy), **team size**, **length of engagement**.

---

## Good questions to ask Atlana / the recruiter (copy-paste friendly)

- “Is this role **embedded with a single client** long-term, or would I move between **accounts**?”
- “Who would be my **day-to-day manager** — Atlana leadership or **client-side** engineering management?”
- “What **overlap hours** do you expect with the **client team’s time zone**?”
- “What is the **primary stack** on this project — **Node/TypeScript**, **Python**, **Java**, mix?”
- “How do you handle **knowledge transfer** and **code quality** — reviews, standards, security training?”
- “What does **success in the first 90 days** look like for someone joining this engagement?”

## Positioning you (honest strengths for *this* model)

Nearshore partners often care that you:

- Communicate clearly in **English** (written + calls)
- Can **integrate** with an existing team — process, git, tickets, reviews
- Own **features vertically** when asked — UI + API + data (your Trackd story)
- Are calm about **integration-heavy** work (email, CRMs, third-party APIs — your freelance story)

---

## Don’t assume

- **Atlana ≠ the end client.** The product you build may be **the client’s brand**.
- **Small partner** can mean **high exposure** — you may wear multiple hats; ask if that excites or worries you and answer accordingly.
